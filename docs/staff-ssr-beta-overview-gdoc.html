Problem Statement

Current staff rounding processes are frequently bottlenecked by high manager span of control and administrative overhead, leaving many clinicians without meaningful check-ins for weeks. This gap creates a feedback vacuum where critical issues regarding burnout, safety, and departmental culture are often lost, forgotten due to recency bias, or withheld due to a lack of psychological safety. Furthermore, chronic labor shortages have forced leaders into frontline roles, effectively eliminating the protected time required for traditional, face-to-face rounding. Consequently, health systems remain reactive and blind to systemic trends, lacking a continuous, real-time stream of actionable insights to support their workforce.

The Opportunity: A Continuous Listening Strategy

We have the opportunity to move beyond episodic, scheduled interactions and implement a continuous listening strategy. By meeting staff where they are — on their mobile devices, at their workstations, and in their breakrooms — we transform rounding from a top-down administrative task into an always-on cultural asset.

  1. Democratize access: QR codes and localized URLs remove the gatekeeper from the feedback loop. Every staff member, regardless of shift or location, has a 24/7 channel to raise their hand at the moment of need.
  2. Standardize competency: Ad hoc checks (e.g., Code Blue protocol validation) can be deployed instantly across the system.
  3. Real-time intervention: Automated logic triggers immediate alerts to HR, Facilities, or Leadership based on staff input.
  4. Harness anonymity: Optional anonymous mode unlocks feedback on culture, safety near-misses, and systemic frustrations that face-to-face rounding cannot access.

Target Outcomes & Success Metrics

  • Increased feedback volume — more unique staff touchpoints per month compared to manual rounding (use-case dependent)
  • Reduction in time-to-resolution — gap between a barrier being reported and its documented fix
  • Improved retention — correlation between high Staff SSR engagement and lower departmental turnover (long-term at scale)
  • Regulatory readiness — digital audit trail of competency checks and acknowledgements

Staff Self-Service Rounding Product

Staff SSR is designed to meet clinicians where they are — at workstations, breakrooms, or on personal devices.

Core solution capabilities

  • Multichannel access: One organization-wide QR code and vanity URL (generic across all staff SSR programs)
  • Secure authentication: Staff enter first name, last name, email, and employee ID to match responses to staff profiles
  • Anonymous when it matters: Optional anonymous mode at the script/program and CP level — staff are authenticated to show correct surveys, but identifying info is not saved on the interaction
  • Flexible programs: Check-ins, satisfaction surveys, new hire rounds, exit interviews, and more
  • Closed-loop intervention: Structured questions trigger issue panels, alerts, and flags to HR, Facilities, or Leadership
  • Same platform, same reporting: Data flows into Staff SSR Insights dashboards and AI Rounding Summaries — not a siloed survey tool
  • Multilingual: Supports all languages that Patient SSR supports, including Spanish

Implementation Details

Implementation Requirements & Considerations

Implementation requirements

  • SFTP connection established with CipherHealth, regularly sending updated HRIS staff files (minimum weekly; more frequent preferred for accuracy)
  • Staff roster current in Evolve so authentication matches active employees

Implementation considerations

  • Staff access questions 24/7 by scanning a QR code or opening a URL in any browser — no CipherRounds app download required
  • QR code: CipherHealth provides one QR code for the organization; place at breakrooms, nursing stations, and shared workstations. Generic — does not change by survey or location
  • URL: CipherHealth provides a custom vanity URL for email campaigns and intranet bookmarks. Generic across programs
  • Beta scope: Start with 1–2 departments at a single facility
  • White labeling: UI can be customized with your brand name, colors, and logo
  • Scripting: Questions and surveys customized to your use case. During beta, customers can have no more than 2 scripts
  • Service recovery: Questions can trigger alerts and issues (SMS, email, or pager). Issues reviewed from Evolve dashboards
  • Anonymity: Optional per survey/program

Customer resources

  • HRIS file owner — ensure CipherHealth receives required HRIS fields and file frequency prevents terminated employees from accessing surveys
  • EC or PC — decide scope, use case, alerting workflows, and script questions; assist Product with requirement gaps and usability feedback; provide white-labeling assets; support staff awareness

Beta participation time commitment

Beta participation runs 3–6 months.

  • Initial kickoff (scope, use cases, scripts, workflows) — 1 hour
  • Weekly workflow design meetings — ~4 hours
  • Stakeholder socialization and awareness — ~2 hours
  • Stakeholder training — ~1 hour
  • Beta testing — daily/weekly staff submissions (depends on rounding culture)
  • Weekly then biweekly feedback sessions — ~8 hours over 2–3 months

Beta Testing Phases & Timeline

Phases are generally sequential. Timeline estimates are directional and subject to project planning between parties.

Phase 1 — Initiate Kick-off with operational stakeholders. Confirm goals, pain points, scope, and success metrics.
Phase 2 — Design Design survey questions, workflows, alerting, issues, and flags. Decide whether anonymous surveys are permitted.
Phase 3 — Configure CipherHealth builds finalized survey and workflow items in Evolve. Customer begins socializing Staff SSR and beta awareness.
Phase 4 — Testing Test survey flow, staff authentication, and anonymous mode (if enabled) on live devices.
Phase 5 — Training Live remote training plus education materials (overview slide, hype flyer, and configuration guide).
Phase 6 — Launch Staff SSR goes live with QR codes placed and programs activated. Weekly/biweekly check-ins gather feedback. Product team maintains an issues, enhancements, and feedback log.
